# WHITE PAPER: The Logic of Lived-Experience Curricula in Trauma Education

**Bridging Clinical Theory and Human Reality**

## The Problem: The "Clinical Gap"
Traditional trauma education often relies on abstract clinical concepts that can be alienating to individuals with Complex PTSD (CPTSD). This "Clinical Gap" leads to disengagement, high dropout rates, and a failure to translate academic knowledge into sustainable behavioral change.

## The Solution: Case Study Methodology
"What Really Happened" uses a **Single-Subject Case Study Methodology**. By grounding every clinical concept—from hyperarousal to relational trauma—in the sustained examination of one documented life, the curriculum provides a concrete, narrative-driven framework for understanding trauma.

## Alignment with Clinical Standards
Our curriculum is designed to complement, not replace, established Evidence-Based Practices (EBP):

1. **Cognitive Processing Therapy (CPT) Alignment:** Our sessions on "The War on Reality" and "Shame vs. Responsibility" mirror CPT's focus on identifying and challenging stuck points and cognitive distortions.
2. **Somatic Experiencing (SE) Alignment:** The curriculum's "Somatic-Minimalist" design and sessions on "Your Brain on Trauma" focus on nervous system regulation and physical safety, core tenets of SE.
3. **Trauma-Informed Care (TIC) Principles:** Every session is built on the five pillars of TIC: Safety, Trustworthiness, Choice, Collaboration, and Empowerment.

## Strategic Value for Institutions
- **For the VA:** Reaches Veterans who are resistant to traditional "talk therapy" by providing a peer-led, narrative entry point.
- **For Rehabs:** Addresses the root trauma that drives addiction, reducing the "Relapse Loop."
- **For Community Groups:** Provides a clinical-grade curriculum that can be facilitated by non-clinicians, increasing institutional capacity without increasing costs.
