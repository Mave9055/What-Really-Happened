Executive Summary

What Really Happened (WRH) delivers a structured, facilitator-led CPTSD education model for institutional pilot settings. The system combines curriculum fidelity, safety protocols, and an auditable software layer to support regulated implementation environments.

Core Competencies

  • CPTSD curriculum development and facilitator scripting
  • Pilot program implementation and deployment checklists
  • Trauma-informed advocacy frameworks and institutional language
  • Safety and escalation protocol design
  • Outcome and measurement framework preparation
  • Software portal architecture for controlled session delivery

Programmatic Pillars

1) Programmatic Offerings

Multi-session trauma education lectures, facilitator manuals, and pilot-ready curriculum packaging for CPTSD group learning.

2) Trauma-Informed Advocacy

Institutional reform messaging, survivor-protective language, and social change framing suitable for grant/federal review.

3) Corporate & Technical Solutions

Software delivery, technical documentation governance, and a placeholder lane for electronics, repair, and smart-home proposals as diversified service offerings.

Past Performance Snapshot

  • Produced and documented a pilot-ready multi-session trauma curriculum with structured facilitator delivery.
  • Maintained a role-based documentation architecture for developers, facilitators, and administrative reviewers.
  • Built a web portal prototype supporting curriculum access and session navigation for implementation pilots.
  • Established safety, escalation, measurement, accessibility, and deployment controls in versioned documentation.

Narrative & Lived Experience

WRH includes a narrative-informed approach grounded in lived experience storytelling, including the Outlaw Gospel stream and creative writing components, to strengthen trauma-informed advocacy context while preserving facilitator-led safety boundaries.
