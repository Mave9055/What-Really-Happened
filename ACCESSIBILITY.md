# WRH Accessibility

## Overview

WRH is designed to be understandable, culturally respectful, and usable across diverse literacy, cognitive, sensory, and neurodivergent needs.

## Who it is for

Facilitators, program managers, and adaptation teams deploying WRH in real-world service environments.

## How to use

Use this file when adapting session delivery, materials, and facilitation methods.

---

## Reading level and language design

- Target plain-language delivery (approximately 6th–8th grade reading level where feasible)
- Define terms in-session and in handouts
- Avoid jargon unless immediately explained in plain language

## Language access

- Provide translated participant materials where possible
- Use interpreters for live sessions when needed
- Validate concepts with community reviewers before large-scale rollout

## Trauma-informed design choices

- Predictable session flow and repeated grounding
- Opt-out/pass options to reduce coercion pressure
- Non-shaming language and strengths-based reframing

## Neurodivergence accommodations

- Allow movement breaks and sensory regulation tools
- Offer visual agenda and explicit transitions
- Provide written + verbal instructions for activities

## Hearing and visual accommodations

- Captioning for virtual sessions and videos
- High-contrast slides and readable font sizes
- Accessible digital files compatible with screen readers

## Literacy accommodations

- One-page summaries with icon-supported cues where possible
- Facilitator read-aloud option for core handouts
- Reflection options beyond writing (verbal check-in, drawing, keyword prompts)

## Delivery environment

- Quiet room options and reduced sensory triggers where possible
- Clear exits and participant control over seating/positioning
- Camera-optional participation in virtual settings when clinically appropriate

## Limits and disclaimers

Accessibility adaptation does not replace clinical judgment. Sites remain responsible for ADA and local accessibility compliance.
