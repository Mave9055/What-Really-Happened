# WRH Outcomes and Measures

## Overview

This document defines practical outcomes and lightweight measurement options for WRH implementations.

## Who it is for

Program evaluators, facilitators, implementation leads, and proposal teams.

## How to use

Administer brief pre/post items at Session 00 and Session 12 (or completion point), then review aggregate trends.

---

## Priority outcomes (3-5)

1. **Nervous-system state recognition**
2. **Use of regulation strategies in daily life**
3. **Reduction in shame/defectiveness beliefs**
4. **Increase in help-seeking and boundary-setting confidence**
5. **Program engagement/completion**

## Suggested pre/post items (example)

Use a 0-4 scale (Never, Rarely, Sometimes, Often, Very Often).

### Outcome 1: State recognition
- I can identify when I am becoming dysregulated.
- I can tell the difference between overwhelm, shutdown, and grounded states.

### Outcome 2: Regulation strategy use
- I used at least one regulation tool in the past week.
- I can return to a workable state faster than before.

### Outcome 3: Shame reduction
- I believe my survival responses mean I am broken. *(reverse scored)*
- I can describe my responses as adaptation, not personal failure.

### Outcome 4: Help-seeking and boundaries
- I can ask for support before I hit crisis.
- I can say no or set limits without extreme guilt.

### Outcome 5: Engagement
- Sessions attended / sessions offered
- Completion status (completed, partial, withdrew)

## Yes/No quick checks

- In the last week, did you use a grounding skill at least once? (Y/N)
- In the last week, did you notice a trauma pattern before acting on it? (Y/N)

## Qualitative reflection

Prompt: **"What really happened for you during this program?"**

Optional follow-up prompts:
- What changed in how you understand your reactions?
- What skill do you trust most now?
- What support do you still need?

## Data and privacy

- Collect minimum necessary data.
- Use de-identified participant IDs for reporting.
- Report only aggregate outcomes in external summaries.
- Do not store PHI in general-purpose project repositories.
- If digital collection is implemented, define retention and access controls in site policy.

## Limits and disclaimers

These measures support program improvement and proposal reporting. They are not diagnostic instruments.
