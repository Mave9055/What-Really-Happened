# WRH Evidence Base

## Purpose

This document outlines the theoretical and clinical foundations informing the design of the What Really Happened (WRH) program.

WRH is a psychoeducational, facilitator-led trauma learning system designed to translate established trauma science into structured group learning experiences.

---

## Core Theoretical Foundations

WRH draws from the following evidence-based domains:

### 1. Nervous System Regulation
- Polyvagal Theory (Porges)
- Window of Tolerance (Siegel)

WRH sessions focus on identifying activation states:
- Hyperarousal (fight/flight)
- Hypoarousal (freeze/shutdown)
- Regulated engagement

---

### 2. Trauma Adaptation Theory
Trauma responses are understood as adaptive survival strategies rather than pathology.

WRH reframes:
- avoidance → protective strategy
- emotional numbing → overload response
- hypervigilance → threat adaptation

---

### 3. Parts-Based / Structural Models
- IFS-adjacent concepts (non-clinical adaptation)
- Protective vs vulnerable self-states

WRH uses accessible language:
- “protector patterns”
- “survival roles”
- “emotional parts”

---

### 4. Somatic Awareness
- Body-based awareness of emotional state
- Interoception and sensation tracking
- Grounding and stabilization techniques

---

## Session Mapping (Example)

- Session 1 → Survival adaptation + safety framing
- Session 3 → Nervous system states (window of tolerance)
- Session 7 → Dissociation and protective absence
- Session 14 → Fight/flight/freeze responses

Each session is designed to reinforce recognition of internal state patterns.

---

## What WRH Is

- Psychoeducational framework
- Facilitator-led group curriculum
- Trauma literacy and stabilization support system

---

## What WRH Is Not

- Clinical diagnosis tool
- Standalone psychotherapy replacement
- Crisis intervention system
- Medical treatment protocol

---

## Summary

WRH integrates established trauma science into a structured educational format designed for safe, guided, group-based learning environments.
