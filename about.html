The Curriculum

About the Curriculum

What Really Happened: A Trauma Education Series was built from one life. Built for the people who circled the building to find the back entrance. Built because silence stopped working.

The curriculum is not a wellness brand. It is not a therapy program. It is a serious, clinical, human document about what Complex PTSD actually looks like from inside the nervous system that lives it — and a curriculum built to teach that to anyone who needs to understand it.

The design matches the material: direct, unsparing, specific, and alive. Every clinical concept is grounded in a specific event, relationship, or moment from a documented life. Every session is built to be facilitated — the room is where the co-regulation happens. The facilitation is load-bearing.

The Person

About Daniel Lingar

Daniel Lingar is the author of From the Storm to the Fire, the memoir that serves as the primary source material for the curriculum. He is the founder of Capitol Contracts LLC and the curriculum developer for What Really Happened: A Trauma Education Series.

The case study that forms the foundation of the curriculum is his own life. The credential is not a degree. The credential is the work and the survival. The curriculum was built from the specific, documented moment-to-moment choice to stay alive when staying alive made no logical sense — and from the understanding that this choice, multiplied across a nervous system shaped by trauma, is the mechanism by which healing becomes possible.

Daniel is available for facilitator training, institutional consultation, and professional speaking engagements. He brings to this work both the lived experience of Complex PTSD and the clinical understanding of how that experience becomes teachable.

"The people who survived the systems that failed them are not obstacles to institutional improvement. They are the experts the institution needs."
