What This Session Does

This session covers key clinical concepts that build on previous material. It teaches the specific mechanisms of how trauma shapes the nervous system and how understanding these mechanisms opens the possibility of change.

Key Terms

Nervous System
The biological system that processes threat and safety. It operates below conscious awareness and shapes behavior before thought.
Trauma Response
The way a nervous system reacts to threat. Fight, flight, freeze, or fawn are the four primary responses.
Window of Tolerance
The zone where the nervous system can function. Outside that zone is hyperarousal or hypoarousal.
Dysregulation
When the nervous system becomes activated beyond its window of tolerance. Symptoms can include panic, rage, dissociation, or shutdown.
Co-regulation
The process by which one nervous system helps regulate another. The facilitator's calm presence helps participants' nervous systems settle.
Integration
The process of bringing fragmented parts of experience back together. Integration is the goal of healing.
