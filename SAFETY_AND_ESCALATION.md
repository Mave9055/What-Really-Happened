# WRH Safety and Escalation

## Overview

WRH prioritizes participant safety by combining trauma-informed facilitation with clear escalation boundaries and referral pathways.

## Who it is for

Facilitators, supervisors, and host organizations delivering WRH.

## How to use

Review before launch, before each cohort, and during facilitator onboarding.

---

## Do / Don't boundaries

### Do
- Keep pacing predictable and grounded.
- Offer pass/step-out options without penalty.
- Use non-pathologizing language.
- Pause content when participants are visibly dysregulated.
- Document and escalate high-risk indicators promptly.

### Don't
- Pressure disclosure of traumatic events.
- Treat WRH as crisis treatment or psychotherapy.
- Continue content while participant safety is in question.
- Promise confidentiality beyond legal/ethical limits.

## Not appropriate as stand-alone support when:

- Active psychosis with impaired reality testing
- Acute suicidality or recent suicide attempt without active clinical care
- Homicidal ideation or imminent violence risk
- Severe intoxication/withdrawal requiring medical stabilization

In these cases, defer to emergency/clinical protocols.

## Crisis protocol (minimum site standard)

1. **Stabilize the room:** pause session, orient, breathing/grounding.
2. **Assess urgency:** determine if immediate danger is present.
3. **Escalate:** involve on-site clinician/supervisor or emergency services.
4. **Warm handoff:** connect participant to crisis line/mobile crisis/ER as needed.
5. **Document:** incident facts, actions taken, follow-up plan.
6. **Debrief:** facilitator/supervisor review within 24 hours.

## Mandated reporting

Sites must follow applicable federal/state/local laws for mandatory reporting (e.g., abuse, neglect, imminent harm). WRH facilitators should be trained on local policy before delivery.

## Local crisis integration requirements

Before launch, each site must define:
- 988 and local crisis resources
- Emergency contacts and escalation chain
- After-hours pathway
- Transportation/safety support procedures

## Facilitator safety

- Use co-facilitation or immediate consult access for high-complexity cohorts.
- Cap back-to-back sessions.
- Require post-session decompression and supervision for difficult incidents.

## Limits and disclaimers

WRH is psychoeducation and must be integrated with clinical/crisis resources for higher-risk participants.
