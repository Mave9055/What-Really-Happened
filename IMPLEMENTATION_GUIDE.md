# WRH Implementation Guide

## Purpose

This guide describes how to implement the WRH program in real-world organizational settings.

WRH is designed for structured, facilitator-led deployment in group environments.

---

## Minimum Staffing Model

- 1 trained WRH facilitator (required)
- 1 co-facilitator or support staff (recommended)
- Access to external clinical support pathway (required)

---

## Recommended Group Structure

- 6–12 participants per group
- 12 core sessions + optional integration session
- Weekly delivery (recommended cadence)

---

## Participant Suitability Criteria

Suitable for individuals who:

- Experience trauma-related stress patterns
- Can participate in group-based discussion
- Are not in acute psychiatric crisis

Not suitable without clinical oversight:

- Active psychosis
- Unmanaged suicidal crisis
- Severe cognitive instability without support system

---

## Deployment Timeline (30-Day Model)

### Week 1: Preparation
- Train facilitators
- Review safety protocols
- Establish referral pathways

### Week 2: Intake
- Participant screening
- Baseline assessment
- Group formation

### Week 3–5: Program Delivery
- Sessions 1–12 delivered
- Weekly facilitator debriefs

### Week 6: Integration
- Post-program assessment
- Outcome review
- Participant reflection synthesis

---

## Safety Requirements

Every deployment must include:

- Crisis escalation pathway (local or national)
- Facilitator grounding/decompression protocol
- Clear stop criteria for participant safety

---

## Fidelity Model

To maintain program integrity:

- Sessions must be delivered in sequence
- Core language must remain consistent
- Facilitator improvisation must not alter core structure

---

## Outcomes Tracking

Minimum recommended metrics:

- Pre/post self-report (stress regulation awareness)
- Participant qualitative reflection
- Facilitator observational notes

---

## Integration Guidance

WRH is designed to complement:

- Clinical therapy
- Case management systems
- Community support programs

It is not a replacement for clinical treatment.

---

## Summary

WRH implementation is designed to be structured, repeatable, and safe across institutional environments while maintaining flexibility for organizational context.
