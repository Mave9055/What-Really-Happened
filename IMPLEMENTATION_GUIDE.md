# WRH Implementation Guide

## Overview

This guide describes how an organization can implement WRH as a trauma psychoeducation track within existing behavioral health, veteran, or reentry services.

## Who it is for

- Program directors
- Clinical supervisors
- Group facilitators
- Contract and operations teams

## How to use

Use this document during pre-launch planning, staffing, and pilot delivery.

---

## Program model

- **Format:** 12 core sessions + Session 00 intake/anchor
- **Cadence:** 1–2 sessions/week
- **Group size:** 6–15 participants recommended
- **Delivery:** In-person or virtual

## Recommended staffing

Minimum:
- 1 lead facilitator trained in trauma-informed group delivery
- 1 support role (co-facilitator, case manager, or peer specialist)

Recommended facilitator background:
- Experience with trauma-exposed populations
- Skill with de-escalation and grounding
- Supervision pathway to licensed clinician for consultation

## Caseload assumptions

Per facilitator (typical):
- 1–3 cohorts concurrently depending on complexity and site infrastructure
- No more than two WRH sessions back-to-back in one day

## Intake and participation criteria

Inclusion examples:
- Adults seeking trauma education and self-regulation skills
- Participants able to engage in structured, paced group content

Exclusion / defer-to-clinical-care examples:
- Acute suicidality without active parallel clinical care
- Active psychosis or severe mania that prevents group participation
- Unmanaged withdrawal or medical instability requiring higher level of care

See `SAFETY_AND_ESCALATION.md` for details.

## Integration with existing services

WRH works best when connected to:
- Individual therapy and/or psychiatry
- Case management
- Substance use recovery supports
- Veteran service navigation

Suggested referral loop:
1. Intake screen
2. WRH participation
3. Clinical handoff for high-risk participants
4. Post-program care planning

## 30-day deployment checklist

### Week 1
- Finalize staffing and supervision plan
- Confirm crisis pathways and local emergency contacts
- Train facilitators on curriculum and boundaries

### Week 2
- Dry-run Session 00 and escalation drills
- Finalize consent and participant-facing language
- Prepare handouts and attendance/outcomes tracking forms

### Week 3
- Launch pilot cohort (Session 00 + Sessions 01–02)
- Conduct immediate debrief after each session

### Week 4
- Continue session sequence
- Review pre/post early indicators
- Adjust pacing, supports, and referral pathways

## Documentation and QA

Track:
- Attendance/completion rates
- Self-reported regulation skill use
- Safety incidents and response timeliness
- Facilitator fidelity notes (adherence to pacing and grounding)

## Limits and disclaimers

WRH is psychoeducation. It is not a substitute for clinical diagnosis, psychotherapy, medication management, or emergency intervention.
