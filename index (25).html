A Psychoeducational Curriculum

What Really Happened is a psychoeducational group curriculum, not a clinical treatment program. It does not diagnose, prescribe, or provide therapy. It teaches the mechanisms of Complex PTSD — its neurological foundations, its relational consequences, its behavioral presentations, and its treatment logic — through the sustained examination of one documented life.

The curriculum is organized in five parts. Part I traces how trauma gets built into a nervous system in early development. Part II examines what it does to adult relationships. Part III follows the coping strategies — substance use, overwork, performance — that the nervous system deploys when regulation becomes impossible through conventional means. Part IV maps the internal architecture of a nervous system in crisis. Part V turns outward — toward what can be learned late, what systems failed and how, and what the revolution requires if it is going to be one.

The methodology is the case study. Every clinical concept is grounded in a specific event, relationship, or moment from the memoir. Abstraction fails where specificity succeeds. A person can memorize the definition of hypervigilance and miss it in a room, but cannot encounter a body that circled an entire building to avoid the front entrance without understanding something about what a nervous system does when proximity has been encoded as threat since before language.
