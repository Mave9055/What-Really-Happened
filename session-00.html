Introduction

What This Session Does

Session 00 is the foundation. It establishes the clinical frame, builds safety, and explains why this curriculum exists. This is not a therapy group. This is not a support group. This is a psychoeducational curriculum built to teach the mechanisms of Complex PTSD through the sustained examination of one documented life.

The session covers the ground rules, the confidentiality agreement, the commitment to the material, and the specific ways that the room will function as a container for difficult material. It names what will happen if someone becomes dysregulated, what resources are available, and how the group will move through the 18 sessions together.

The opening grounding exercise establishes the nervous system regulation that will frame every session. The closing grounding exercise teaches the participant how to return to baseline after engaging with clinical material.

Key Terms

Psychoeducational
A curriculum designed to teach information and mechanisms, not to provide therapy or clinical treatment. The focus is on understanding, not on healing.
Clinical Frame
The explicit agreement about what this group is, what it is not, what the boundaries are, and what happens when those boundaries are tested.
Co-regulation
The process by which one nervous system helps regulate another. The facilitator's calm, grounded presence helps the participants' nervous systems settle.
Dysregulation
When the nervous system becomes activated beyond its window of tolerance. Symptoms can include panic, rage, dissociation, or shutdown.
Grounding
A technique to bring the nervous system back to the present moment and the body. Used at the opening and closing of every session.
Confidentiality
What is said in the room stays in the room. This is the explicit agreement that allows people to speak truth.

Discussion Prompts

1 What brought you here? What are you hoping to understand or learn from this curriculum?
2 What does safety mean to you? What conditions do you need in order to speak truth?
3 Have you ever been in a group where the confidentiality agreement actually held? What was different about that space?
4 What does it mean to you that this is not a therapy group? How does that change what you expect?

Write about a time when you felt truly safe in a group. What was present? What was the facilitator doing? What made you believe that what you said would be held with care?
