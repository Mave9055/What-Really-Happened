"A concept you can only hold in your head is a concept you cannot use in your body." Session 02

Methodology: Why a Case Study

The curriculum is built around a single documented life — a published memoir that traces one nervous system from premature birth through chronic complex trauma, addiction, relationship destruction, system collapse, and the specific, non-linear process of rebuilding.

Every clinical concept is grounded in a specific event, relationship, or moment from the source material. The methodology is not abstraction. Abstraction fails where specificity succeeds. A person can memorize the definition of hypervigilance and miss it in a room, but cannot encounter a body that circled an entire building to avoid the front entrance without understanding something about what a nervous system does when proximity has been encoded as threat since before language.

Session Architecture

Each session follows this consistent clinical architecture. The facilitation is load-bearing. The room is where the co-regulation happens.

The Five Parts

Session 00 — Introduction

Building safety and establishing the clinical frame. Welcome, Safety, and Why This Course Exists.

Sessions 01–02 — Part I: How It Gets Built Into You

Early development and trauma formation. The Blueprint of Survival. The Masks We Wear.

Sessions 03–04 — Part II: How It Destroys Your Relationships

Adult relationships and relational consequences. When Love Hurts. The War on Reality.

Sessions 05–07.5 — Part III: How You Tried to Cope

Coping strategies and regulation mechanisms. Relapse Isn't a Betrayal. What Healing Actually Looks Like. The Breaking Point. Shut Up, I Have Work Tomorrow (Deep Dive).

Sessions 08–12 — Part IV: The Internal Battlefield

Crisis architecture and neuroscience. Rewriting the Legacy. Shame vs. Responsibility. Your Brain on Trauma. The Anatomy of a Spiral. Crisis, Suicide Risk, and Staying Alive (Safety Protocol).

Sessions 13–16 — Part V: Changing the World Around You

Integration and systemic change. The Lessons Nobody Taught Us. Speaking Truth to Power. The Crucial Question. The Revolution Must Be Trauma-Informed.

When Love Hurts

Participants will understand how Complex PTSD disrupts attachment patterns and produces relational dynamics that re-enact original harm, including the mechanics of trauma bonding.

Part II

The War on Reality

Participants will be able to identify gaslighting, reality distortion, and dissociation as trauma responses rather than character defects, and distinguish between functional memory and trauma-fragmented recall.

70 min

05

Part III

Relapse Isn't a Betrayal

Participants will understand addiction and compulsive behavior as nervous system regulation strategies, reframe relapse as clinical information rather than moral failure, and identify the shame spiral as the primary driver of continued use.

70 min

06

Part III

What Healing Actually Looks Like

Participants will develop a realistic, non-linear model of recovery that accounts for regression, window of tolerance, and the difference between symptom management and actual nervous system change.

70 min

07

Part III

The Breaking Point

Participants will recognize the internal and external conditions that precede a crisis event, identify their personal warning signs, and understand why the nervous system reaches a breaking point rather than gradually declining.

70 min

07.5

Part III

Shut Up, I Have Work Tomorrow

Participants will examine the high-functioning trauma presentation in depth — how occupational performance masks dysregulation, why productivity becomes a dissociative strategy, and what the regulation tax actually costs before a shift begins.

Deep Dive 90 min

08

Part IV

Rewriting the Legacy

Participants will identify inherited trauma narratives passed through family systems, distinguish between what was taught and what is true, and begin constructing an accurate account of their own history.

70 min

09

Part IV

Shame vs. Responsibility

Participants will differentiate between shame (identity-level condemnation) and responsibility (behavior-level accountability), understand why shame prevents the change it demands, and practice accountability language that does not collapse into self-destruction.

70 min

10

Part IV

Your Brain on Trauma

Participants will understand the neurological mechanics of the trauma response — amygdala hijack, prefrontal cortex suppression, HPA axis dysregulation — in plain language applicable to their own experience.

70 min

11

Part IV

The Anatomy of a Spiral

Participants will be able to map the sequential stages of a trauma spiral, identify their personal entry points, and recognize the difference between a spiral in progress and a nervous system that has already bottomed out.

70 min

12

Part IV

Crisis, Suicide Risk, and Staying Alive

Participants will understand the neurological and relational conditions that produce suicidal ideation, learn to distinguish passive ideation from active planning, and identify both personal safety resources and facilitator response protocols.

Safety Protocol 70 min

13

Part V

The Lessons Nobody Taught Us

Participants will identify the specific developmental skills — emotional regulation, boundary-setting, conflict repair — that trauma interrupted, and understand why their absence is a gap in education, not evidence of deficiency.

70 min

14

Part V

Speaking Truth to Power

Participants will practice naming harm accurately to institutional and relational power structures, understand the neuroscience of why truth-telling feels dangerous, and develop language for advocacy that does not require self-destruction.

70 min

15

Part V

The Crucial Question

Participants will engage directly with the question of meaning-making after sustained trauma — not resolution or forgiveness, but the construction of a livable account of what happened and why survival matters.

70 min

16

Part V

The Revolution Must Be Trauma-Informed

Participants will analyze how institutional systems misread trauma symptoms as behavioral or moral failures, identify the feedback loop between trauma response and legal/social consequence, and articulate what trauma-informed design requires from the systems they move through.
