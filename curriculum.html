Methodology: Why a Case Study

The curriculum is built around a single documented life — a published memoir that traces one nervous system from premature birth through chronic complex trauma, addiction, relationship destruction, system collapse, and the specific, non-linear process of rebuilding.

Every clinical concept is grounded in a specific event, relationship, or moment from the source material. The methodology is not abstraction. Abstraction fails where specificity succeeds. A person can memorize the definition of hypervigilance and miss it in a room, but cannot encounter a body that circled an entire building to avoid the front entrance without understanding something about what a nervous system does when proximity has been encoded as threat since before language.

Session Architecture

Each session follows this consistent clinical architecture. The facilitation is load-bearing. The room is where the co-regulation happens.

The Five Parts

Building safety and establishing the clinical frame. Welcome, Safety, and Why This Course Exists.

Early development and trauma formation. The Blueprint of Survival. The Masks We Wear.

Adult relationships and relational consequences. When Love Hurts. The War on Reality.

Coping strategies and regulation mechanisms. Relapse Isn't a Betrayal. What Healing Actually Looks Like. The Breaking Point. Shut Up, I Have Work Tomorrow (DEEP DIVE).

Crisis architecture and neuroscience. Rewriting the Legacy. Shame vs. Responsibility. Your Brain on Trauma. The Anatomy of a Spiral. Crisis, Suicide Risk, and Staying Alive (SAFETY PROTOCOL).

Integration and systemic change. The Lessons Nobody Taught Us. Speaking Truth to Power. The Crucial Question. The Revolution Must Be Trauma-Informed.
