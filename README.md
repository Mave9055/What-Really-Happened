# What Really Happened (WRH)

WRH is a **12-session trauma psychoeducation program (+ Session 00 intake anchor)** designed for adults impacted by complex trauma, chronic dysregulation, and shame-based survival patterns. It is built from lived experience and trauma-informed facilitation practices.

## Overview

- **Program type:** Psychoeducation curriculum (not psychotherapy)
- **Dosage:** 12 sessions + 1 intake/anchor session
- **Session length:** 30–35 minutes (intake is longer)
- **Delivery settings:** Community groups, reentry programs, veteran-serving programs, recovery settings, telehealth-compatible psychoeducation cohorts
- **Primary artifact:** `facilitator-manual.md`

## Who it is for

WRH is intended for organizations serving:

- Veterans and veteran families
- Justice-involved and reentry populations
- Adults in recovery programs (when paired with clinical supports)
- Survivors of chronic interpersonal trauma

Recommended participant profile:

- Able to engage in structured psychoeducation
- Not currently requiring immediate emergency psychiatric intervention
- Has access to parallel clinical/crisis support if high-risk factors are present

## 12-session structure at a glance

- **Session 00:** Intake anchor and psychological safety container
- **Sessions 01–12:** Trauma patterns, nervous-system responses, relationships, shame, grief, and recovery-oriented behavior change

See `facilitator-manual.md` for full sequence and scripts.

## WRH in the continuum of care

WRH is designed to support — not replace — clinical services.

- Can be used as a psychoeducational track in a broader care pathway
- Can improve engagement and shared language before/during therapy
- Should be integrated with local case management, behavioral health, and crisis resources

## 30-day implementation path (organizational)

- **Week 1:** Orientation, facilitator onboarding, risk pathway setup
- **Week 2:** Intake workflow dry run, consent language review, pilot enrollment
- **Week 3:** Deliver Sessions 00–02, perform facilitator debrief
- **Week 4:** Continue delivery, review outcomes snapshots, adjust supports

Detailed implementation guidance is in `IMPLEMENTATION_GUIDE.md`.

## Limits and disclaimers

- WRH is **psychoeducation**, not psychotherapy or emergency mental health treatment.
- WRH is not a substitute for psychiatric evaluation, substance detox care, or crisis intervention.
- Sites must define escalation pathways before launch.

See `SAFETY_AND_ESCALATION.md` for required safeguards.
