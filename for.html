Who This Curriculum Is For

Five deployment contexts. One curriculum. Designed for institutions that need trauma literacy now.

Recovery & Residential Treatment Programs

The curriculum is designed to run as a standalone psychoeducational group series within existing treatment infrastructure. It does not require clinical staff to facilitate — it requires trained facilitators who can follow the script, hold the container, and connect participants to clinical support when the material surfaces acute need.

The curriculum addresses addiction as a regulatory strategy rather than a moral failure. It names the shame spiral as the mechanism most likely to produce relapse. It provides a framework for understanding what sobriety without regulation actually costs.

"Relapse isn't a betrayal. It's the nervous system saying: I don't have another way to regulate yet."

Correctional & Reentry Settings

The legal echo chamber analysis in Session 16 — the documented feedback loop between trauma symptoms, institutional misreading, and compounding legal consequence — is built from direct experience of the warrant system.

The curriculum is designed to be comprehensible to people who have moved through courts and corrections as the person whose freeze response produced the failure to appear, not as the observer of that process. It teaches the neuroscience of why the brain underwater cannot execute the compliance the court is requiring.

"The brain underwater cannot execute the compliance the court is requiring."

Community Mental Health & Social Services

The curriculum is appropriate for clients in case management, peer support, or outpatient contexts who are not in active crisis but who have never had the mechanisms of their own nervous system named in plain language.

The Plain Version methodology — no clinical jargon required, all terms defined at first use, no diagnostic gatekeeping — is designed for general audiences. The DEEP DIVE expansions are designed for clinician-level groups. The same script serves both.

"Naming the regulation tax accurately is not self-pity. It is the prerequisite for addressing it."

Clinician & Professional Training

Trauma literacy — understanding what a fawn response is, why the amygdala hijack happens before the thinking brain catches up, what the regulation tax costs before a shift begins — belongs in any setting where people are expected to function.

A clinician who has moved through all 18 sessions understands why shame-based intervention fails, why the brain underwater cannot execute the compliance the court is requiring, why "peace boring, sign healing — not wrong" is a clinical observation rather than an inspirational statement.

"A concept you can only hold in your head is a concept you cannot use in your body."

Workforce & Peer Support Programs

Trauma literacy belongs in employer-sponsored wellness contexts, peer support specialist training, and community education settings.

The curriculum is deployable in any setting where people are expected to function under conditions that were shaped by lives the conditions didn't account for. It teaches the neuroscience of why the regulation tax is real and why naming it accurately is not weakness.
